## 7 Conclusion

The central claim of this paper is that agency cost, not raw capability, is the binding constraint on recursive AI systems in this implementation. The reason is structural: when the same optimizing process generates output and evaluates that output, the system acquires an incentive to fabricate compliance rather than produce real progress. The M-Form response is to separate generation from evaluation with a deterministic enforcement floor that the agent cannot negotiate away.

The contribution is therefore not a promise of perfect alignment. It is a narrower and more defensible claim: hard-gated principal separation can bound the most destructive form of recursive self-deception, and it can do so in a way that is auditable and reproducible. The evidence is real but scoped. The architecture works in one system; it is not yet a universal law supported by broad replication.

That scope limitation does not weaken the paper's central importance. If the same governance logic recurs across the model layer, the evaluator layer, and the organizational layer, then recursive AI improvement is not just a capabilities problem. It is also a firm-design problem. The M-Form is the current answer this system has for that problem.
