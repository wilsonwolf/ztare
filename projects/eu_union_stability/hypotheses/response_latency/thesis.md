# Response Latency Thesis

The EU's main structural weakness is not simply incomplete fiscal union; it is institutional response latency. Crisis governance that depends on discretionary bargaining, multi-state approval, and treaty-adjacent improvisation may be too slow for high-velocity modern shocks.

The stronger claim is therefore conditional: even if the EU can eventually improvise stabilizing instruments, a system that responds at political bargaining speed may fail when shocks propagate at market speed. Under that condition, the core fragility is temporal mismatch rather than institutional incompleteness in the abstract.
