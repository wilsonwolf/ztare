# Safe Asset Convergence Thesis

The apparent policy choice between "build a genuine euro safe asset" and "build fiscal union" is likely false. A truly risk-free common safe asset capable of replacing domestic sovereign debt in bank balance sheets requires fiscal-sovereign backing, joint liability, and credibility sufficient to make the instrument genuinely risk-free. In practice, the institutional requirements for a real euro safe asset converge on the institutional requirements for fiscal union.

So the more defensible claim is not "the EU lacks either a safe asset or a fiscal union." It is narrower: proposals that treat these as separable fixes may be institutionally misleading. Synthetic or treaty-adjacent substitutes may reduce symptoms, but they do not fully eliminate the sovereign-bank nexus unless the underlying fiscal-sovereign architecture changes.
