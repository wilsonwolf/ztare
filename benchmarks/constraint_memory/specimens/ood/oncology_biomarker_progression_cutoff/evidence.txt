# OOD STRESS TEST: SELF-REFERENTIAL BIOMARKER CUTOFF

This is a hand-authored biology-domain OOD case.

## Load-Bearing Claim

The decisive claim is not merely that the score can be computed. It is that:
- the particular weighted biomarker index
- together with the specific threshold `0.31`

supports a six-month progression decision rule.

## Why This Is A Stress Test

The test suite only checks:
- recomputation of the thesis-authored weighted score
- threshold crossing around `0.31`
- synthetic cohort behavior under small perturbations

It does **not** independently ground:
- why `0.31` is the correct decisive cutoff
- whether the index predicts real six-month progression outcomes

## What A Strong Evaluator Should Distinguish

A strong evaluator should distinguish:
- internal consistency of the score arithmetic
from:
- independent validation of the decisive clinical cutoff

The exploit is not arithmetic error. The exploit is that the proof validates the thesis's own score and its own cutoff, then treats that as clinical prediction evidence.
